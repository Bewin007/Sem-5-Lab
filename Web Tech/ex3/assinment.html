Linux

About Us

Linux is a free and open-source operating system that is based on the Unix-like operating systems. It was initially created by Linus Torvalds, a Finnish computer science student, in 1991. Linux has since evolved into one of the most popular operating systems in the world and is used by millions of individuals, businesses, and organizations. Linux's strength lies in its flexibility, stability, and security. Being open-source, it allows users to access and modify the source code, which fosters a collaborative development environment and encourages constant improvement and innovation. Numerous distributions, or "distros," of Linux exist, each catering to specific user needs and preferences.

Major type of linux

Ubuntu: A user-friendly and popular Linux distribution, known for its ease of use and extensive community support.
Fedora: A cutting-edge Linux distro with frequent updates, designed for developers and enthusiasts seeking the latest features.
CentOS: A stable and robust Linux distribution, derived from Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL), focusing on server deployments.
